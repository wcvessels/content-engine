# Client brief

I record a lot. Team sessions, walkthroughs, training calls, one-off "let me show you how this works" screen shares. The knowledge is all in there, but it is trapped in hours of video nobody is going to rewatch. I need those recordings turned into polished, shareable documents: educational guides, standard operating procedures, knowledge-base articles, release notes.

The problem with generic AI summaries is that they lie. They invent steps that were never demonstrated, drop the specific numbers and names that actually matter, and smooth over the parts where someone said "actually, do it the other way." When I hand a teammate a guide, every claim in it has to be something that was really said or really shown. I cannot ship a document that confidently describes a button that does not exist.

So I want a folder system, not a one-shot prompt. Feed it a transcript plus the screenshots from a recording, and it produces a faithful, polished deliverable: clean reader-facing prose with no timestamps or jargon, screenshots placed next to the step they illustrate and cropped to the relevant on-screen content (drop the webcam tile, keep the screen), and a brand-consistent PDF and interactive HTML. Internally, every claim must be traceable back to the source, with a QA gate that catches anything invented before it ships.

And it has to be a factory: when I need a new document type, I add a lane without rebuilding the machine.
