Getting Started on Ramp as a Manager

A standard operating procedure for a manager's first run on Ramp: accept your invite, work the home task list, view cards, submit receipts and codes, manage your team, request cards, and submit reimbursements.

content-engine (Will Vessels) · 2026-06-30

For: A new manager or approver setting up and operating their Ramp account for the first time.

Purpose

This procedure walks through how to get started on Ramp as a manager. It covers your first account access and the core tasks a manager handles: the home task list, viewing your cards, submitting receipts and codes, managing your team, requesting cards, and submitting reimbursements. As a manager you can also complete these tasks on behalf of your team members.

Scope

The recording demonstrates the manager tasks listed in the procedure below. It does not state an explicit boundary for what falls outside this procedure.

Roles and owners

You, the manager, perform every step in this procedure. As a manager you can also submit items and request cards on behalf of a team member. Card and reimbursement requests are issued only after approval.

Before you start

Before you startYou need an email invite from Ramp to create your account. To receive reimbursements by direct deposit, you also need to add a personal bank account, and your company must have reimbursement submissions enabled in Ramp.

The procedure

The recording walks through the following manager tasks in this order. Do them as you need them; the sequence here follows the recording.

Accept your invite and sign in

  1. Open the invite email you receive from Ramp.
  2. Click Accept invite to create your account.
  3. Log in. You land on your home page, which is your task list.

Work from your home page

Your home page is your task list. Everything that needs your approval shows up here, whether it is a transaction, a reimbursement, a funds request, a bill, or more. Your own transactions that need attention appear here too.

Use the quick tabs at the top to toggle between all of your tasks and your own expenses. You can also review approvals from the Inbox tab for a consolidated view of pending items.

View your card details

On the right-hand side you can view the cards issued to you.

  1. Select your card to reveal the full card details.
  2. Use the copy boxes to grab the card number, expiration date, CVV, or billing address when you make a virtual purchase.
  3. Click Actions in the top right for options to manage and protect your card.

Submit receipts, memos, and codes on a transaction

Depending on how your company sets up its policies, you may need to submit receipts and memos and code your card transactions and reimbursement requests. You can complete these required fields for yourself or on behalf of a team member to keep expenses compliant.

  1. Click into a transaction for a detailed view of the expense.
  2. Upload a receipt: click to upload, or drag and drop. You can also submit receipts by text, in the Ramp app, or by email.
  3. Fill out the memo.
  4. Use the drop-down or the text field to fill in any required accounting codes.
NoteIf a transaction is missing a receipt, memo, or accounting code, Ramp prompts you to fill it in.

Invite and manage your team

From the Team tab you can invite users, see everyone who reports to you, and edit their profiles. To invite a new team member:

  1. Click Invite in the top right corner.
  2. Enter the employee's email address.
  3. Select their manager, location, and department.
  4. Select their role on Ramp.
  5. If they have a team reporting to them, toggle on Will they manage others.
  6. Click Send invite in the bottom right to issue the invite.

To edit a user, click the user's name, then Edit profile in the top right. You can change their department, location, manager, and role. You can also lock all of their cards, deactivate their account, or permanently terminate their account from this window.

WarningLocking cards, deactivating, and permanently terminating an account are all available from the edit window. The recording labels termination as permanent, so confirm before you use it.

Request a card

As a manager you can request additional cards for yourself or your team.

  1. Click Request and select your card type.
  2. Under Who will be the owner, select yourself or the team member you are requesting the card for.
  3. Enter the card label, the limit, and a description of why you are requesting it.
  4. Click Request in the bottom right to submit.

All card and reimbursement requests show up on your home page and are issued after approval.

Add your bank account for reimbursements

Reimbursements are typically paid by direct deposit, so you are prompted to add your bank account to receive the ACH payment.

  1. Click your initials in the top right.
  2. Go to Settings, then Banking.
  3. Add your personal bank account details.

Submit an out-of-pocket reimbursement

An out-of-pocket reimbursement is when you used your personal credit card or cash for a work-related expense and your employer pays you back. You can request one for yourself or for a team member if your company has enabled reimbursement submissions in Ramp.

  1. From your home page, click Request reimbursement.
  2. If you are asked, provide your place of residence so the request complies with local laws and processes accurately.
  3. Select the out-of-pocket reimbursement type.
  4. Upload a receipt, or select No receipt. Dragging and dropping a receipt auto-fills the merchant, amount, and date; you can also enter these manually.
  5. Complete the memo and accounting fields.
  6. Submit the request, or it saves as a draft until you are ready. You can also delete the request.

Submit a mileage reimbursement

A mileage reimbursement is when you used your personal vehicle for business travel and your employer pays you back for gas.

  1. From your home page, click Request reimbursement.
  2. Click New mileage.
  3. Enter your start and end location, and add any stops if needed.
  4. Ramp calculates the distance and applies the IRS mileage rate automatically.
  5. Submit the request once all the details are filled out.

Decision points

These are the choices the recording shows. Only the options stated in the recording are listed.

  • Submit for yourself or a team member. You can complete expenses, request cards, and request reimbursements for yourself or on behalf of a team member.
  • Will they manage others? When inviting a team member, toggle this on if they have a team reporting to them.
  • Out-of-pocket or mileage. Choose the reimbursement type: out-of-pocket for personal-card or cash spend, mileage for business travel in your personal vehicle.
  • Receipt or no receipt. On an out-of-pocket reimbursement, upload a receipt or select No receipt.
  • Auto-fill or enter manually. Drag and drop a receipt to auto-fill the merchant, amount, and date, or enter them manually.
  • Submit or save as a draft. Submit the request, or it saves as a draft until you are ready. You can also delete it.

Risks and things to watch

  • Missing fields hold up compliance. If a transaction is missing a receipt, memo, or accounting code, Ramp prompts you to fill it in to keep the expense compliant.
  • Account termination is labeled permanent. From the edit-user window you can lock cards, deactivate an account, or permanently terminate it. Confirm before you terminate.

References and tools mentioned

  • Receipt submission channels: upload in the app (click or drag and drop), text, the Ramp app, and email.
  • Reimbursement payment: typically by direct deposit (ACH) to your personal bank account.
  • Mileage rate: Ramp applies the IRS mileage rate automatically, based on the distance it calculates.

Open questions

  • Is reimbursement submission available? Requesting reimbursements through Ramp depends on whether your company has enabled it.
